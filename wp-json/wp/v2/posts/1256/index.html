By huge coincidence our dates for visiting Perth and Western Australia coincided with the annual Motor Neuron Disease conference being held in Perth.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I was very excited when I first realised this months ago and booked my ticket to attend. So for 4 days this week I have commuted into the city centre and attended the Allied Health Professions (AHP) day and the main medical and research conference.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I’ve learnt such a lot about current research in current practice, cause and treatment of MND: when I return to the UK I’ll hopefully be able to share lots of interesting information with my fellow AHPs at Airedale NHS Trust.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I was thrilled to see Emily Plowman presenting as I have been following her research and trying to promote it in clinical practice within my West Yorkshire networks.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

And it was lovely one lunch time to be able to go out and meet Mike and the kids for a brief play before heading back in! <\/p>\n\n\n\n

I met some great people during the conference, one doctor lives in Sydney and we’re hoping to meet up for a walk when we visit there in February! I also bumped into a Consultant Neurologist who I used to work with at Airedale before he emigrated to Australia. He was also at the conference and it was lovely to catch up with him and reminisce! I spent a lot of time at the poster session and through good luck managed to get a personal explanation from Nora who went on to win the Biomedical Poster Prize.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

On 4\/12\/19 it was the innaugral Global Walk to D’Feet MND<\/a>, raising awareness with a sea of MND t-shirts walking 5 Kms along the Swan River! Martha joined me for the walk and was my special reporter on the spot!<\/p>\n\n\n\n
